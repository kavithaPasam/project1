Economic Tribal Welfare

In India, the tribal population has historically been marginalized and excluded from mainstream economic development. To address this issue, the government of India has implemented several economic schemes and programs for the welfare of tribal communities.

Schemes

Tribal Sub Plan (TSP)

Pradhan Mantri Van Dhan Yojana

Institutional Support For Development And Marketing Of Tribal Products


1. Tribal Sub Plan

The Tribal Sub Plan (TSP) is a scheme launched by the Government of India in 1974 to allocate funds for the welfare and development of tribal communities in the country. The TSP is a part of the annual budget of the central and state governments, with funds being earmarked specifically for the development of tribal areas and the welfare of tribal communities. The TSP is planned and implemented by the Ministry of Tribal Affairs, Government of India, in collaboration with the concerned state governments.

  • The TSP scheme aims to promote the socio-economic development of tribal communities by supporting various initiatives such as education, healthcare, livelihoods, and infrastructure development. The scheme aims to bridge the gap between the development needs of tribal communities and the general population and ensure that the tribal communities receive the benefits of the government's development policies.
  • Under the TSP scheme, the central and state governments allocate funds for various projects and programs for the development of tribal areas and the welfare of tribal communities. These funds are utilized for the establishment of schools, hospitals, skill development centers, water supply and sanitation systems, and other developmental activities.

    • Overall, the TSP scheme plays a crucial role in promoting the development and welfare of tribal communities in India, and it has been instrumental in improving the living standards and socio-economic conditions of the tribal communities in the country.

2. Van Dhan Yojana

Van Dhan Yojana (VDY) is a scheme launched by the Government of India in 2018 to provide for the development of tribal communities through the creation of self-help groups and the establishment of value chains for forest produce. The scheme aims to promote sustainable livelihoods for tribal communities by facilitating the establishment of small-scale forest-based enterprises and value addition of non-timber forest produce. Under the scheme, the government provides training, capacity building, and market linkage support to tribal entrepreneurs engaged in forest-based enterprises. VDY aims to promote inclusive and sustainable development of tribal communities while also promoting the conservation of forest resources. The scheme is being implemented by the Ministry of Tribal Affairs, Government of India.

3. Institutional Support for Development and Marketing of Tribal Products

Institutional Support for Development and Marketing of Tribal Products is a scheme launched by the Government of India to provide support for the development and marketing of tribal products. The scheme aims to promote the socio-economic development of tribal communities by facilitating the establishment of tribal enterprises engaged in the production and marketing of tribal products.

Under the scheme, the government provides financial assistance, training, capacity building, and market linkage support to tribal entrepreneurs engaged in the development of tribal products.

  • Some of the Schemes are

  1. Tribal Cooperative Marketing Development Federation of India (TRIFED): TRIFED is a national level apex organization functioning under the Ministry of Tribal Affairs, which aims to promote the economic development of tribal communities through the development and marketing of their products. TRIFED provides support to tribal artisans and entrepreneurs by organizing exhibitions, providing training and capacity building, and creating market linkages for their products.
  2. National Scheduled Tribes Finance and Development Corporation (NSTFDC): The NSTFDC is a government-owned corporation that provides financial assistance to tribal entrepreneurs for the establishment and expansion of their businesses. The corporation offers various schemes, such as term loans, margin money schemes, and venture capital assistance, to help tribal entrepreneurs access credit and finance for their businesses.
