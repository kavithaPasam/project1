Health Care Tribal Welfare

The Government of India has launched several schemes and programs to improve the health status of tribal populations in the country. These schemes aim to provide equitable and accessible healthcare services to the tribal population, who often face socio-economic and cultural barriers to accessing healthcare.

1. Swasthya Tribal Health & Nutrition Portal

Swasthya Tribal Health & Nutrition Portal is a web-based platform developed by the Ministry of Tribal Affairs, Government of India, to provide information and services related to health and nutrition to the tribal communities of India. The portal aims to improve the health and well-being of tribal communities by providing easy access to reliable and accurate health-related information and services.

The official website

https://swasthya.tribal.gov.in/home

  1. The portal provides a range of health and nutrition-related content, including articles, videos, and infographics. Users can browse the content by clicking on the different categories or by using the search bar. The portal also offers various services to the tribal communities, including telemedicine, e-pharmacy, and online appointment booking. To avail these services, users need to create an account on the portal and provide their personal and health-related information.
  2. The Swasthya Tribal Health & Nutrition Portal is available in multiple languages and can be accessed from any device with an internet connection. The portal also allows users to provide feedback on the content and services provided and seek support by using the contact details provided on the portal.


The Swasthya Tribal Health & Nutrition Portal is an important initiative by the Indian government to improve the health and well-being of tribal communities by providing easy access to reliable and accurate health-related information and services.

2. National Health Mission

NHM aims to provide universal access to equitable, affordable, and quality healthcare services to all, including tribal populations. Under the NHM, special emphasis is given to providing healthcare services in remote and underdeveloped areas where tribal communities live.

The official website

Some of the key NHM tribal welfare schemes are:

  1. National Tribal Health Mission (NTHM): The NTHM is a flagship program of the Ministry of Health and Family Welfare aimed at improving the health status of tribal populations. It focuses on strengthening the health infrastructure, providing essential drugs, and improving the availability of human resources for health in tribal areas.
  2. Mobile Medical Units (MMUs): The MMU scheme is a mobile healthcare delivery system that provides basic healthcare services to remote and inaccessible areas, including tribal areas. It aims to reduce the burden of communicable diseases and improve maternal and child health.

These are some of the key NHM tribal welfare schemes that are aimed at improving the health and well-being of tribal communities in India. The government is continuously working towards the implementation and expansion of these schemes to ensure that every tribal person in India receives adequate healthcare services.
